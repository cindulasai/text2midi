### Sample Lesson:

```
EDUCATIONAL INSIGHT: Chord Progressions

Explanation:
────────────
Chords are built by stacking notes. In this composition, we use:
  • Major triads (root + major 3rd + perfect 5th) - Bright, happy
  • Minor triads (root + minor 3rd + perfect 5th) - Dark, sad
  
The progression I-IV-V-I creates powerful harmonic movement:
  I (Tonic)     = home, stable, resolution point
  IV (Subdominant) = moving away, preparation
  V (Dominant)  = tension, leading back
  I (Tonic)     = return home, satisfaction

Why This Matters:
──────────────
This progression appears in thousands of great songs because it creates 
a natural harmonic cycle. V→I is one of the strongest musical resolutions.

Example from Your Composition:
─────────────────────────────
In your C major progression:
  C major (happy, home) → F major (preparing) → G major (tension) → C major (resolution)
  
This creates satisfying harmonic flow that listeners enjoy unconsciously.

Further Learning:
──────────────
1. Practice: Play this progression on piano/guitar in different keys
2. Analysis: Identify I-IV-V-I in songs you love
3. Creation: Compose your own chord progression using these roles
```
